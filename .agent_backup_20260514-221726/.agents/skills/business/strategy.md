# Product Strategy Skill Pack

This skill pack provides frameworks for defining product vision, strategy, business models, and value propositions.

## 1. Product Strategy Canvas
A comprehensive 9-section framework to define the strategic direction of a product.
1. Vision & Mission
2. Target Audience
3. Problem/Need
4. Value Proposition
5. Key Features
6. Differentiators / Moat
7. Business Model & Pricing
8. Go-to-Market Strategy
9. Key Metrics (North Star)
*Prompt Pattern:* "Draft a Product Strategy Canvas for [Product Concept]."

## 2. Value Proposition (JTBD)
Define value based on the Jobs-to-be-Done framework.
- **Who**: The specific target user.
- **Why**: The core 'job' they are hiring the product to do.
- **What Before**: How they solve the problem today (the status quo).
- **How**: How the proposed product solves the problem better.
- **What After**: The desired emotional and practical state after using the product.
- **Alternatives**: Direct and indirect competitors.
*Prompt Pattern:* "Define a 6-part JTBD value proposition for [Target User] doing [Task]."

## 3. Lean Canvas & Business Model
For new startups or major new product lines. Focuses on identifying high-risk areas of a business model early.
- Problem, Solution, Key Metrics, Value Proposition, Unfair Advantage, Channels, Customer Segments, Cost Structure, Revenue Streams.

## 4. Monetization & Pricing Strategy
Brainstorming pricing models and willingness-to-pay validation.
- Explore Freemium, Usage-based, Tiered SaaS, One-time, etc.
- Analyze price elasticity and competitor positioning.
*Prompt Pattern:* "Suggest 3-5 monetization strategies for [Product type] and how to test them."

## 🛠️ Implementation Checklist
- [ ] Is the vision inspiring, actionable, and tied to business goals?
- [ ] Does the value proposition clearly articulate the 'Job to be Done'?
- [ ] Is the target audience narrowly defined enough to be actionable?
- [ ] Are the pricing models aligned with the perceived value of the target user?
